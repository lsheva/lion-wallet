# Branding — Name Candidates

This document captures the naming exploration for the wallet. The final name should align with the product philosophy: free, fast, open-source, lightweight, trustworthy, no tracking, no commercial features. Safari-first, cross-browser future. Simple for beginners, advanced for developers.

## How to Judge Conflicts

Every short, cool English word has been used somewhere in crypto. There are 30,000+ tokens, 500+ wallets, and thousands of protocols. A zero-conflict name either doesn't exist or is too obscure for anyone to remember.

The real test is three questions:

1. **Is there a wallet with this exact name?** Not a protocol, not a token — a wallet extension or app.
2. **Do they serve the same users?** A Waves-chain wallet or a Solana trading bot does not compete with a Safari EVM wallet.
3. **Would someone searching "[name] wallet" find us or them?** The SEO/discoverability question.

A $LION token on Cronos with minimal market cap is not a real conflict. A "Leo Wallet" Chrome extension with 100K users is.

The standard pattern in wallets is: **the brand is one word, the product is "[brand] Wallet."** Trust → Trust Wallet. Phantom → Phantom Wallet. Rainbow → Rainbow Wallet. This naturally differentiates from tokens or protocols sharing the same word.

## Top 5 — Detailed Breakdown

### 1. Seal — *mark of authenticity*

**Score: 34/40** — highest overall.

A wax seal authenticates a document. It proves the sender is who they claim to be. That is exactly what a crypto wallet does — it signs transactions, proving your identity and intent. The word also gives you a second logo option: the animal, which is friendly, memorable, and distinctive.

- **Metaphor:** Every transaction you approve gets your seal. The wallet is your seal — your mark, your identity, your signature on the blockchain.
- **UX signal:** Trust, finality, approval. "Seal of approval." When something is sealed, it's done. Non-crypto users instantly understand this.
- **Crypto connection:** Strong — signing/sealing transactions is the core function of a wallet. "Sealed" also implies tamper-proof, immutable.
- **Visual identity:** Two directions — a wax seal stamp (premium, authoritative, embossed circle) or a seal animal (playful, friendly, unique). Either works at any size. The animal gives a MetaMask-fox-level mascot. The stamp gives a Phantom-level premium mark.
- **Weakness:** SealVault exists (iOS TestFlight app) and Seal by MystenLabs (Sui infra). Neither is a browser extension wallet, but "Seal" has some namespace proximity to key management concepts. The oil/gas company Seal is not well-known enough to matter.
- **Conflict verdict:** No "Seal Wallet" browser extension exists. SealVault is a different product on a different platform.
- **Logo idea:** Minimal wax seal impression with an "S" or Ethereum diamond embossed in the center. Or a geometric seal (animal) in profile.
- **Color palette:** Deep crimson wax (`#B91C1C`), parchment cream (`#FFFBEB`), gold accent (`#D4AF37`). Or ocean blue (`#0369A1`) with white if using the animal direction.
- **Tagline ideas:** "Signed. Sealed. Yours." / "Your signature on the blockchain." / "Every transaction bears your seal."

### 2. Lion — *king of the savanna*

**Score: 33/40**

The lion is THE safari animal. Apple used "Lion" for macOS 10.7 and "Mountain Lion" for 10.8. Every person on earth knows what a lion is. A non-crypto person hears "Lion Wallet" and immediately gets it: strong, protective, premium. No explanation needed.

- **Safari connection:** Maximum. First animal anyone associates with an African safari.
- **Apple heritage:** macOS Lion, Mountain Lion. Feels native to the ecosystem.
- **Visual identity:** A stylized lion head in profile. Mane as radiating lines or a minimal geometric mark. Iconic at 16px, recognizable at 128px. Prints beautifully on app icons, extension badges, and splash screens.
- **UX signal:** Approachable, not intimidating. Lions are majestic but not aggressive. They protect the pride.
- **Weakness:** No crypto double meaning. The name doesn't communicate "lightweight" or "transparent." This gap can be covered by the tagline and product experience rather than the name itself.
- **Conflict:** Clean. No "Lion Wallet" exists. $LION token on Cronos is tiny and irrelevant. Leo Wallet (Aleo chain, 100K users) uses a different name.
- **Logo idea:** Minimal lion head in profile, single-weight line art or flat geometric. Gold/amber accent on dark background.
- **Color palette:** Amber gold (`#D97706`), deep mane brown (`#44311D`), warm savanna cream (`#FEF3C7`). Alternatively, keep the existing indigo (`#6366F1`) for a more modern/tech feel against the lion mark.
- **Tagline ideas:** "Your keys. Your pride." / "Guard your assets." / "The wallet with nothing to hide."

### 3. Hinge — *the invisible mechanism*

**Score: 33/40**

A hinge enables a door to open and close. Without it, you can't enter or leave. It's a small, invisible mechanism that makes everything else work. You never think about a hinge — until it's missing.

- **Metaphor:** The wallet is the hinge between you and the blockchain. It doesn't draw attention to itself. It just enables access. This is the most philosophy-aligned name in the entire list.
- **UX signal:** Effortless, reliable, invisible. A good hinge is one you never notice. That's exactly how a wallet should feel — it gets out of your way.
- **Philosophy fit:** The strongest of all candidates. Minimal, essential, no decoration. It does one thing perfectly and disappears.
- **Apple feel:** Simple English word, physical, clean. Feels like something Apple would name a feature.
- **Weakness:** No safari/desert connection. "Hinge" doesn't evoke the wild, the sun, or the journey. Also, Hinge (the dating app) is well-known — but it's a completely different market, and nobody would confuse a dating app with a crypto wallet.
- **Conflict verdict:** Completely clean in crypto. No wallet, no token, no protocol.
- **Logo idea:** Abstract hinge shape — two planes meeting at a pivot. Or a door slightly ajar, light coming through. Geometric, minimal, architectural.
- **Color palette:** Brushed steel (`#94A3B8`), deep charcoal (`#1E293B`), warm white. Mechanical, precise, modern.
- **Tagline ideas:** "Your door to Web3." / "Open anything." / "The pivot between you and the blockchain."

### 4. Tiger — *fast, bold, unmistakable*

**Score: 31/40**

Striking, instantly recognizable, and visually unforgettable. Tiger stripes are one of the most distinctive patterns in nature — a logo that designs itself. Apple used "Tiger" for macOS 10.4.

- **Safari connection:** Strong. Big cat, wild, safari animal.
- **Apple heritage:** macOS Tiger. One of the most beloved macOS releases.
- **Visual identity:** Stripes. Black and amber/orange. A tiger face or a single stylized stripe pattern. Bold, energetic, impossible to confuse with any other brand.
- **UX signal:** Fast, energetic, sharp. Might feel slightly more aggressive than Lion.
- **Weakness:** Same as Lion — no crypto meaning. tigerwallet.io exists but is a low-quality trading site, not a real competitor.
- **Logo idea:** Stylized tiger face with bold stripe accents, or abstract stripe pattern forming a "T."
- **Color palette:** Burnt orange (`#EA580C`), obsidian black (`#0A0A0A`), white. High contrast, energetic.
- **Tagline ideas:** "Fast by nature." / "No clutter. No claws." / "Stripped down to what matters."

### 5. Rhino — *armored, unstoppable, iconic*

**Score: 31/40**

The rhino is impossible to ignore. Thick-skinned, armored, built to last. The horn is one of the most recognizable silhouettes in nature. It communicates security without needing to say it.

- **Safari connection:** Maximum. One of the Big Five safari animals.
- **Visual identity:** The horn. A rhino silhouette or a minimal horn mark. Heavy, grounded, unbreakable.
- **UX signal:** "Nothing gets through." Communicates security and durability. A rhino doesn't care about noise or trends — it just moves forward.
- **Weakness:** Rhinos are associated with being heavy and slow — exactly the opposite of "lightweight and fast." This is the key tension. Also, Rhino.fi is well-known in DeFi ($8B volume) which could cause some brand proximity.
- **Logo idea:** Geometric rhino head in profile, or a minimal horn shape.
- **Color palette:** Gunmetal grey (`#4B5563`), warm earth (`#92400E`), chalk white. Heavy, grounded.
- **Tagline ideas:** "Thick-skinned. Zero bloat." / "Built to last." / "Nothing gets in."

### Detailed Breakdown

#### Hinge — *the pivot point* ⭐

A hinge enables a door to open and close. Without it, you can't enter or leave. It's a small, invisible mechanism that makes everything else work.

- **Metaphor:** The wallet is the hinge between you and the blockchain. You can't open the door to Web3 without it. It's not the door, not the room — it's the thing that connects them.
- **UX signal:** Effortless, mechanical, reliable. A good hinge is one you never think about — it just works. That's the product philosophy.
- **Philosophy fit:** Strong. A hinge is minimal, essential, no decoration. It does one thing perfectly.
- **Visual identity:** Abstract hinge shape — two rectangles connected by a pivot point. Or a door slightly ajar (invitation, access). Geometric, clean.
- **Approachable:** Everyone knows what a hinge is. No explanation needed.
- **Apple feel:** Simple English word, physical, clean. Apple would use this.
- **Conflict:** Clean in crypto. Hinge dating app is a completely different market.
- **Color palette:** Brushed silver/steel (`#94A3B8`), deep charcoal (`#1E293B`), accent warm white. Mechanical, precise, modern.
- **Tagline ideas:** "Your door to Web3." / "Open anything." / "The pivot between you and the blockchain."

#### Seal — *mark of authenticity*

A wax seal authenticates a document. It proves the sender is who they claim to be. That is exactly what a crypto wallet does — it signs transactions, proving identity and intent.

- **Metaphor:** Every transaction you approve gets your seal. The wallet is your seal — your mark, your identity, your signature.
- **UX signal:** Trust, authority, finality. When something is "sealed," it's done. "Seal of approval."
- **Philosophy fit:** Moderate. Conveys authenticity and trust, less about lightweight.
- **Visual identity:** A wax seal stamp impression — circular, embossed, premium. Or a stylized seal (the animal) — playful, friendly, memorable.
- **Approachable:** Everyone knows both meanings. The animal version is especially friendly and fun.

#### Glide — *effortless motion*

To glide is to move smoothly and continuously with minimal effort. No friction, no resistance, no noise.

- **Metaphor:** Using this wallet should feel like gliding — you don't fight the interface, it carries you. Safari "explores" the web; Glide describes how you move through it.
- **UX signal:** The strongest UX name in the list. "Glide" IS the product experience: smooth, silent, frictionless. A non-crypto person hears "Glide Wallet" and thinks: easy, premium, modern.
- **Philosophy fit:** Strong. Gliding requires minimal energy — lightweight. No turbulence — no bloat. Effortless — simple UI.
- **Visual identity:** A flowing curve, a wing shape, a smooth arc. Something that suggests motion without effort. Elegant, minimal.
- **Approachable:** Everyone knows "glide." Zero learning curve on the name.
- **Apple feel:** Very Apple. Smooth, premium, one-word. Apple would absolutely name something "Glide."
- **Conflict:** Glide (buildwithglide.com) is a crypto payment SDK for wallet funding and checkout flows. It's infrastructure for developers, not a consumer wallet. Different product, but same crypto space. "Glide Wallet" as a brand would need to coexist with this.
- **Color palette:** Soft lavender (`#A78BFA`), cloud white (`#F8FAFC`), deep navy (`#1E1B4B`). Airy, smooth, premium.
- **Tagline ideas:** "Glide through Web3." / "Smooth. Secure. Yours." / "No friction. Just flow."

#### Latch — *secure and hold*

A latch is a simple fastening mechanism — lift, click, done. It secures a door or gate with no keys, no codes, no ceremony. The most intuitive lock there is.

- **Metaphor:** The wallet latches onto your keys and doesn't let go. Simple security, nothing fancy, just reliable.
- **UX signal:** Simple, mechanical, trustworthy. A latch doesn't fail. You hear the click and you know it's locked.
- **Philosophy fit:** Strong. A latch is the simplest possible security mechanism — no extra parts, no complexity. Minimal by nature.
- **Visual identity:** A latch mechanism — a hook and catch. Or a padlock clasp shape. Geometric, clean, industrial.
- **Approachable:** Everyone knows what a latch is. Doors, gates, suitcases.
- **Apple feel:** Good. Simple mechanical word, clean English.
- **Conflict:** Latch.io exists as a blockchain interoperability protocol and has `@latch-wallet` npm packages. It's infra/protocol, not a consumer wallet extension, but the namespace overlap is real — they literally use "latch-wallet" in their package names.
- **Color palette:** Iron grey (`#6B7280`), warm wood brown (`#78350F`), brass accent (`#B45309`). Mechanical, grounded, honest.
- **Tagline ideas:** "Latch onto your keys." / "Click. Locked. Yours." / "Simple security."

#### Tap — *instant access*

Tap to pay. Tap to open. The most intuitive gesture in modern computing.

- **Metaphor:** Interacting with crypto should be as simple as a tap. No friction, no ceremony.
- **UX signal:** Instant, modern, mobile-native. Everyone understands "tap."

#### Compass — *finding your way*

A compass always points true north. It doesn't tell you where to go — it helps you orient yourself.

- **Metaphor:** The wallet helps you navigate the blockchain without telling you what to do. No DeFi promotions, no suggested trades — just clear orientation.

#### Wire — *direct transmission*

A wire carries a signal from point A to point B with no loss. Direct, efficient, no intermediary.

- **Metaphor:** Your transactions go directly to the blockchain. No middleman, no data harvesting, no detours.

#### Shell — *protective layer*

A shell protects what's inside. It's the first line of defense. Also: command line shell (developer culture).

- **Conflict:** **Taken.** Shell Wallet Chrome extension (Cosmos, 10K users). Also Royal Dutch Shell (massive brand).

### Summary — Real-World Objects

Only **Hinge** is fully clean. **Seal** has minor conflicts but nothing in the same product category (no "Seal Wallet" browser extension exists). The rest are taken by existing wallet products.

Hinge is an unusual pick — nobody else in crypto has thought to use it. That's an advantage. It's short, everyone knows the word, and the metaphor is precise: a hinge is a small, essential mechanism that enables access. It doesn't draw attention to itself. It just works.

**Seal** scores highest because it hits the most factors at once: everyone knows the word, it directly describes what a wallet does (signing/sealing), it has a built-in mascot (the animal) OR a premium mark (wax stamp), and "Seal Wallet" is unclaimed. "Signed. Sealed. Yours." writes itself.

**Lion** is the safest, most universally understood pick. Zero conflicts, maximum safari connection, Apple lineage. If brand recognition and approachability are the top priority, this is the one.

**Hinge** is the most philosophy-aligned pick. It's the only name that perfectly mirrors the product ethos: invisible, essential, no decoration, just works. Completely clean in crypto. The tradeoff is no safari connection and the dating app sharing the word (different market entirely).

**Clef** is the most elegant pick. French for "key" — and private keys are the entire foundation of a wallet. The treble clef 𝄞 is a universally recognized symbol that doubles as a ready-made logo. It has the strongest Apple feel of any candidate. The tradeoff is zero safari connection and a dead-but-recent 2FA brand sharing the name.

The choice depends on what story you want the name to tell:

- **"We sign and protect your transactions"** → Seal
- **"We're the king of the safari, the wallet Apple never made"** → Lion
- **"We're the invisible tool that just works"** → Hinge
- **"We're the key to Web3, elegant and essential"** → Clef
